Q1. Is it safe for me to exercise during pregnancy?
Yes. Physical activity during pregnancy is generally safe and beneficial for most women. The Get Active Questionnaire for Pregnancy (GAQ-P) can help identify whether you need to consult a healthcare professional before starting or continuing exercise.

Q2. My doctor or midwife hasn’t told me to be active. Does that mean I shouldn’t be?
Not necessarily. If you are healthy and have an uncomplicated pregnancy, you can choose to be active. If you have complications or concerns, discuss physical activity with your doctor or midwife.

Q3. I’m in pain. What should I do?
Stop the activity. Pain is a signal that something may not be right. Consider slowing down, adapting, or stopping completely, and allow adequate recovery, especially after childbirth.

Q4. What does “moderate activity” mean?
Moderate activity means you breathe a little harder but can still talk comfortably, though not sing. This level varies depending on individual fitness.

Q5. I’m not active. How do I start?
Start gradually. Walking is a simple and popular option. If needed, seek support from a fitness professional qualified in pregnancy and postnatal exercise.

Q6. How do I achieve 150 minutes of activity per week?
You can accumulate activity across the week in any pattern that suits you. Any activity is better than none, even if you don’t reach the full 150 minutes.

Q7. Do I need special equipment?
No special equipment is required for activities like walking. For gym or class-based exercise, a supportive sports bra and appropriate footwear are recommended.

Q8. Will running harm my baby?
No. The baby is well protected in the womb. If you run regularly, adapt your routine as your body changes and ensure proper support.

Q9. Should I pay for a personal trainer?
This is optional. If you choose to hire one, ensure they have appropriate pregnancy and postnatal qualifications. You can also use trusted activity guides to exercise independently.
