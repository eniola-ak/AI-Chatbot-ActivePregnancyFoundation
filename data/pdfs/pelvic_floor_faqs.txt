Q1. Should I be doing pelvic floor exercises?
Yes. Pelvic floor exercises are recommended during pregnancy and after birth once you feel able. Support from a midwife or pelvic health physiotherapist can help ensure they are done safely and effectively.

Q2. What are pelvic floor muscles and why are they important during and after pregnancy?
Pelvic floor muscles support the bladder, bowel, core, and sexual function. During and after pregnancy, these muscles may weaken due to the strain of pregnancy and childbirth. Regular exercises support recovery and function, starting as soon as possible after birth.

Q3. How often should I do pelvic floor exercises during and after pregnancy?
Pelvic floor exercises should be performed regularly during pregnancy and daily for at least 12 weeks after birth. Exercises include slow holds and quick contractions, which can be done throughout the day in different positions.

Q4. What is a Pelvic Health Physiotherapist?
A Pelvic Health Physiotherapist specialises in women’s pelvic health and supports pregnancy preparation, postnatal recovery, and treatment of conditions such as pelvic pain, incontinence, and prolapse.
